The Theory of the Innovative Organization by William Lazonick

An excerpt from William Lazonick’s terrific work “Business Organization and the Myth of the Market Economy”, a book that offers a critique of what a ‘market economy’ is purported to be, through an evaluation of the strategies and economic histories of businesses within what can be called “actually existing capitalism”. Here, at the risk of a small amount of repetition, I shall supplement that discussion by focusing on the nature of fixed costs, the different sources of uncertainty the innovative organization faces, and the relation between organizational capability and technological change.
